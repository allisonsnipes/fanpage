About Parcels

Parcels are a Berlin-based band composed of:

  • TLouie Swain (keyboards)
  • Patrick Hetherington (keyboards)
  • Noah Hill (bass)
  • Anatole "Toto" Serret (drums)
  • Jules Crommelin (guitar)
who migrated from Australia. The band formed in 2014 in high school at Byron Bay, Australia. The group is now based in Berlin, Germany.

Parcels are signed to the French label Kitsuné. Their single "Overnight", released on 21 June 2017, was produced by Daft Punk; who heard them at their first performance in France. Daft Punk invited them to their LA studio, and requested to hear some demo tracks that they'd been working on. Daft Punk was inspired by 'Overnight', and they proceeded to work towards recording and releasing the single with Parcels. In an interview, Parcel's keyboardist Patrick said “First we spent seven days in the studio with them, every day from 12 midday up until 4 in the morning, literally working on it overnight."

The band's first EP, Clockscared, was released on 03-02-2015. Their second EP, Hideout, was released on 01-27-2017, which was entirely computer-produced. Their sound is described as having "an unmistakeable penchant for the 70s; fusing together the old and the new". They describe themselves as "sort of a blend between electro-pop and disco-soul"

On 06-25-2017, they performed at the Glastonbury Festival for the Silver Hayes Pussy Parlure. In September 2017, their first US TV appearance was on Conan where they performed 'Overnight'. On 01-08-2018,they were interviewed by BBC Radio 1 and performed: "Overnight", "Anotherclock", and "Older". On 09-20-2018, they performed "Lightenup" live on Neo Magazin Royale.

On 04-27-2018, the band released a single called "Tieduprightnow". On 07-13-2018, they released another single titled "Bemyself". On 09-06-2018, they released another single called "Lightenup". All three singles were released ahead of their self-titled debut album, which was released on 10-12-2018.
